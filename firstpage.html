Analog Synthesizers, Synthesis and Electronic Music

Analog Synthesizers

A synthesizer is basically an instrument made of different interconnected sections designed to generate and alter the properties of sounds.

Analog synthesizers are made of electronic components arranged in way that the sound produced or altered is directly related to the voltage and current running in the circuitry.

Digital synthesizers include pre-made integrated circuits in it's construction and tend to sound different. Some people argue that analog generated sounds are more pleasing to the ear.


Synthesis

Synthesizing a sound means that we take use some form of signal generator to output a tone so that we can shape its form.

The classical tones that most synthesizers present are made of sine waves, saw waves, triangle waves and square waves. These tones are, then, directed to other parts of the synthesizer so properties - like duration, decay, sustain, frequency filtering and others - can be altered. These alterations are made by specialized modules, built with a specific function in mind.

Here, we are going to give a special attention to Modular Analog Synthesizers due to their incredible versatility, freedom of creation and range from simple to complex sound creation.

In a modular system, the "parts" that generate or alter sound are called Modules. Each module present specific functions and connections so you can route the sound signal as you please.

Let's take a look at the most basic modules:

Module Function Parameters
VCO - Voltage Controlled Oscillator Generate tones Pitch control, fine tuning, CV inputs, FM inputs, waves outputs
VCF - Voltage Controlled Filter Filter frequencies - High, Band or Low Pass Filter Cutoff, Resonance, CV inputs and outputs, LFO inputs
Envelope Generator - ASDR Generator Adjust time of Attack, Sustain, Decay and Release of a sound Attack Time, Sustain Time, Decay Time, Release Time, CV inputs and outputs, LFO inputs
Low Frequency Oscillator - LFO Generate a signal which amplitude varies slowly and in a specific shape Time, shape outputs, CV inputs


Types of Synthesis

There are different approaches to sound synthesis and each uses different processes to produce a sound. Here is a list of the most basic ways to synthesize:

  • Subtractive
  • Additive
  • Granular
  • Sampling
  • Frequency Modulation
  • Amplitude Modulation
  • Wavetable Synthesis
